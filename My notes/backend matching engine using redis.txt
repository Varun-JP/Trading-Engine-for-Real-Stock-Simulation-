We are using a matching engine because **that is what makes a trading platform an actual exchange**, rather than just a dummy dashboard.

Right now, your app is basically a "ghost town." When you click BUY, the message is broadcast to the screen, but nothing actually *happens*. You could say you want to BUY 100 shares of RELIANCE at ₹2500, but who are you buying them from?

The **Matching Engine** is the brain that turns individual intents into real economic events. Here is exactly why your architecture requires it:

---

### 1. It Enforces Market Reality (Price Discovery)

In a real system, you can’t just make up a price and instantly own the stock. For a trade to happen, two people must agree:

* **The Buyer** wants to buy as cheaply as possible.
* **The Seller** wants to sell as expensively as possible.

The matching engine acts as the absolute arbiter. If you submit a `BUY` order at ₹2500, the engine searches the market. If a seller is already sitting there offering it at ₹2500 or lower, **BOOM—a trade is born.** If the cheapest seller is asking for ₹2510, the engine blocks the execution and makes your order wait in line.

---

### 2. It Creates the Data that Feeds Everything Else

Remember how we talked about building live Candlestick charts (OHLC) and training Machine Learning models later?

* Right now, your charts would just be plotting whatever random, fake numbers a user types into the input box. That data is completely meaningless noise.
* With a matching engine, a data point is generated **only when an actual match occurs**. That matched price becomes the **Last Traded Price (LTP)**. Your charts and your ML models will be analyzing *real transaction history*, not unexecuted user typing.

---

### 3. It Simulates Industrial Concurrency

From a purely technical, software engineering perspective, a pass-through pipe doesn't test your backend skills. It's too easy for a server to just forward a text string.

A matching engine forces you to solve real-world complex problems:

* **Race Conditions:** What happens when 500 users all race to buy the exact same block of shares at the exact same millisecond?
* **State Management:** How does Redis instantly keep track of who owns what share in memory while matching trades at microsecond speeds?

---

### In Simple Terms:

Without a matching engine, your project is just a **chat room** where people yell out stock prices.

With a matching engine, your project becomes a **mini-National Stock Exchange (NSE)** that actively pairs buyers with sellers, updates balances, and calculates true market prices in real time.
